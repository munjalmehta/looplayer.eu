🔑 Strategic finding: LoopLayer qualifies as a "DPP Service Provider" under ESPR Art. 2
ESPR Art. 2 defines a "digital product passport service provider" as an independent third party authorised by the economic operator to process DPP data and make it available to entitled actors. This is a formal legal category. LoopLayer should formally register under this definition — it becomes a regulatory credential, not just a product description.
⚠️ Gap identified: Microplastic release is now a mandatory DPP field for textiles
ISO 4484-2:2023 (textiles) quantifies microplastic shedding from textiles. This is explicitly listed in the JRC methodology as an ESPR Annex I mandatory data point. No competitor is currently collecting this at transaction level. LoopLayer can add this as a data field and become the only platform that surfaces it.
📋 Substances of Concern: the DPP is the mandatory disclosure mechanism
ESPR Art. 7(5) requires name, location, concentration and end-of-life guidance for all REACH/SVHC substances per product. The DPP is where this lives. LoopLayer's chemical safety constraint (no claims without lab testing) is correct — but LoopLayer can provide the structured disclosure field and partner with Hohenstein or similar for testing, rather than making the claim itself.

Implementation note: GTIN alignment
ESPR Annex III(c) specifically requires GTIN per ISO/IEC 15459-6. For textiles traded as bulk lots (not consumer products), a GS1 Application Identifier system using batch/lot numbers is appropriate. LoopLayer should register as a GS1 member company to issue compliant GTINs — cost is approximately €250–500/year for a German SME.

DPP Granularity: Batch-Level is Correct for LoopLayer

ESPR Art. 9(2)(d) — delegated act will specify model, batch, or item level. JRC Annex 7 guidance confirms batch is appropriate for textile lots. Source: JRC145830 Annex 7.
🏷️
Model-Level
One DPP per product design. All units share the same passport. Appropriate for identical mass-produced goods (e.g. one hoodie model). LoopLayer lots are too variable for this.
NOT APPROPRIATE
📦
Batch-Level
One DPP per production lot. Units share characteristics based on production conditions — same factory, time period, raw material sourcing. The JRC document explicitly uses "textile lot produced with a particular dye lot" as the canonical batch example.
RECOMMENDED FOR LOOPLAYER
🔍
Item-Level
One DPP per individual item. Warranted only for high-value, long-life products (e.g. batteries, cars). JRC notes this creates "enormous number of passport entries" for mass-produced textile goods — economically unsustainable.
NOT APPROPRIATE
